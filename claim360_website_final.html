Summary: We collect only the data necessary to deliver your investment recovery services. We do not sell your personal information. You can request deletion at any time. This policy applies to all services provided by 360 Degrees Management Services Pvt. Ltd. ("Claim360").

Section 01

Who We Are

360 Degrees Management Services Private Limited, trading as Claim360, is a company incorporated under the Companies Act, 2013, with its registered office in Mumbai, Maharashtra, India. We operate investment recovery, IEPF claim management, NRI services, and related financial advisory services.

For the purposes of the Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023 (DPDPA), we act as the Data Fiduciary for personal data collected through our website (claim360.in), client portal, and ancillary services. Our Data Protection Officer can be contacted at the details provided in Section 11.

Section 02

Information We Collect

We collect personal data that you voluntarily provide to us, data generated through your use of our services, and in limited cases, data from third-party sources with your consent.

Identity & Contact Information:

  • Full name, date of birth, photograph (where required for KYC)
  • Email address, phone number, WhatsApp number
  • Residential address — current and historical
  • PAN card number, Aadhaar number (masked), OCI/PIO card details
  • Passport details (for NRI clients)

Financial & Investment Information:

  • Share folio numbers, demat account details (DP ID and Client ID)
  • Bank account details (IFSC, account number — for credit of recovered assets)
  • Investment portfolio details, share certificates, dividend records
  • Succession certificates, wills, probate documents (where applicable)

Automatically Collected Data:

  • IP address, browser type and version, operating system
  • Pages visited, time spent, referral sources
  • Cookies and similar tracking technologies (see Section 9)
Section 03

How We Use Your Data

We use your personal data solely for the following purposes:

  • To provide investment recovery, IEPF claim, and share management services you have engaged us for
  • To verify your identity and conduct KYC/AML checks as required by applicable law
  • To correspond with registrars, RTAs, IEPF, SEBI, company secretaries, courts, and other regulatory bodies on your behalf
  • To prepare and execute legal documents including power of attorney, affidavits, and transmission requests
  • To send you service updates, case status communications, and required legal notices
  • To send you optional marketing communications (with your explicit consent, which you may withdraw at any time)
  • To comply with our legal and regulatory obligations under SEBI, IEPF, MCA, and DPDPA regulations
  • To improve our website and services through anonymised analytics
  • To respond to regulatory inquiries or legal proceedings

We will never use your data for purposes beyond those disclosed without your prior explicit consent.

Section 04

Legal Basis for Processing

Under the Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023, we process your personal data on the following lawful bases:

  • Consent: For marketing communications, optional data collection, and non-essential cookies
  • Contractual necessity: For all data required to deliver the services you have contracted us for
  • Legal obligation: For KYC, AML, and regulatory reporting requirements
  • Legitimate interests: For security monitoring, fraud prevention, and product improvement — balanced against your rights

For clients in the UK and EU, we additionally comply with the UK GDPR and GDPR respectively, processing data under equivalent lawful bases under those frameworks.

Section 05

Sharing & Disclosure

We do not sell, rent, or trade your personal data to third parties. We share your data only in the following circumstances:

  • Regulatory bodies: IEPF Authority, SEBI, BSE, NSE, MCA, and courts — as required to process your recovery claims
  • Registrar & Transfer Agents (RTAs): KFin Technologies, Link Intime, Bigshare Services — to process share transmissions and demat requests
  • Depository Participants: NSDL, CDSL — for demat account operations
  • Legal professionals: Advocates and notaries engaged specifically for your case, bound by professional confidentiality obligations
  • Technology service providers: Cloud hosting, secure document management, and email services — under strict data processing agreements
  • Law enforcement: Where required by a valid court order or statutory obligation

Important: Any third party we engage is contractually bound to process your data only for the specified purpose and to protect it to the same standard we do. We do not permit third parties to use your data for their own marketing purposes.

Section 06

Data Retention

We retain your personal data for as long as necessary to fulfil the purposes for which it was collected, comply with legal obligations, resolve disputes, and enforce our agreements.

  • Active client records: Duration of the service engagement plus 7 years (as required by Indian financial regulations)
  • KYC & identity documents: 5 years from the end of the client relationship, per PMLA requirements
  • Legal case records: 10 years from case closure (for cases involving court proceedings)
  • Website analytics data: 26 months (anonymised after 13 months)
  • Marketing consent records: Until withdrawn, plus 3 years
  • Enquiry-only records (no engagement): 2 years from initial contact

Upon expiry of retention periods, data is securely deleted or anonymised using industry-standard methods.

Section 07

Security Measures

We implement industry-standard technical and organisational security measures to protect your personal data against unauthorised access, loss, destruction, or alteration:

  • 256-bit SSL/TLS encryption for all data transmitted via our website and client portal
  • AES-256 encryption for data stored at rest
  • Role-based access controls — data accessible only to authorised personnel on a need-to-know basis
  • Multi-factor authentication for all staff accessing client systems
  • Regular security audits and vulnerability assessments
  • Physical security controls at all office locations
  • Staff data protection training and confidentiality agreements
  • Incident response plan with mandatory breach notification procedures

In the event of a data breach that poses a risk to your rights, we will notify you and the relevant Data Protection Board within the timelines prescribed by law.

Section 08

Your Rights

Under the DPDPA 2023 and applicable regulations, you have the following rights regarding your personal data:

  • Right to access: Obtain confirmation of whether we hold your data and receive a copy
  • Right to correction: Request correction of inaccurate or incomplete personal data
  • Right to erasure: Request deletion of your data, subject to our legal retention obligations
  • Right to grievance redressal: Lodge a complaint with our Grievance Officer (Section 11) or the Data Protection Board of India
  • Right to withdraw consent: Withdraw consent for marketing communications or optional data processing at any time
  • Right to nominate: Nominate an individual to exercise your data rights in the event of your death or incapacity

For UK/EU clients, additional rights apply under UK GDPR/GDPR including the right to data portability and the right to object to processing based on legitimate interests.

To exercise any of these rights, contact our Grievance Officer (Section 11). We will respond within 30 days.

Section 09

Cookies & Tracking

Our website uses cookies and similar technologies. We use the following categories:

  • Strictly necessary: Essential for the website to function (login sessions, security tokens). Cannot be disabled.
  • Performance / Analytics: Anonymised data on how visitors use the site (e.g., Google Analytics). Enabled with your consent.
  • Functional: Remember your preferences such as language and region. Enabled with consent.
  • Marketing: Not currently used. We do not serve behavioural advertising on this website.

You can manage your cookie preferences through the cookie consent banner displayed on your first visit, or through your browser settings. Note that disabling strictly necessary cookies may impair functionality.

Section 10

NRI & International Transfers

As a firm serving clients in India, the UK, USA, Canada, Australia, and the UAE, we may transfer personal data across international borders in order to deliver our services. All such transfers are made in compliance with applicable law:

  • For UK clients: Transfers are made under UK GDPR-compliant mechanisms including Standard Contractual Clauses or UK Adequacy Decisions where applicable
  • For EU clients: Transfers comply with GDPR Chapter V requirements
  • For US, Canada, Australia, and UAE clients: Transfers are made under contractual safeguards consistent with the DPDPA and the laws of the respective jurisdiction

Your data may be processed and stored on servers located in India and, where our technology partners operate, in the UK and/or EU (all under appropriate data protection agreements).
